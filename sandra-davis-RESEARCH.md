## CASE SUMMARY

On September 1, 1984, 22-year-old Sandra Kaye Davis was found strangled to death beside a home on Kollock Street in Waycross, Georgia. She had been sexually assaulted before being killed. The small town was shocked by the brutal murder, and the Waycross Police Department immediately requested assistance from the Georgia Bureau of Investigation (GBI).

For 42 years, the case remained unsolved. DNA evidence was collected in 1984, but the technology to analyze it properly didn't exist. As decades passed and DNA technology advanced, the GBI and Waycross PD periodically re-tested the evidence, waiting for science to catch up to justice.

On March 31, 2026—more than four decades after Sandra's death—the GBI announced the arrest of Freddie Lee Granger Jr., age 68, of Ware County. Continued DNA testing had finally linked Granger to the crime. He was charged with one count of rape, one count of aggravated assault, one count of malice murder, and two counts of felony murder. He was booked into the Ware County Jail, and the case that had haunted Waycross for 42 years was finally closed.
