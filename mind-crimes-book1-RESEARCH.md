## 📋 COMPLETE TIMELINE

### **1990: THE MURDER**

**May 26, 1990 - 2:30 PM (Saturday, Memorial Day weekend)**
- Location: Warren residence, Wellington, Florida
- Marlene Warren, 40, returns from grocery shopping
- Pulls white Mercedes into garage
- Doorbell rings
- Opens door to see person in full clown costume

**The Clown:**
- White face paint
- Orange yarn wig
- Polka-dot jumpsuit with silver pom-poms
- Oversized shoes
- Holding: Carnations bouquet + 2 balloons (one "Get Well Soon", one with rainbow)

**The Murder:**
- Clown hands flowers/balloons to Marlene
- Marlene smiles, waves
- Clown pulls .38-caliber revolver
- Single shot to Marlene's face
- Son Joseph Airey (21) witnesses from inside with friends
- Marlene collapses in pool of blood
- Clown runs to white Chrysler LeBaron convertible (stolen earlier that day)
- Speeds away

**May 28, 1990**
- Marlene dies after being taken off life support (2 days after shooting)

**May 30, 1990**
- White Chrysler LeBaron recovered
- Orange wig found inside
- Fibers from clown costume

### **1990-1995: INITIAL INVESTIGATION**

**Immediate Suspects:**
- Sheila Keen (28) - employee at Michael Warren's used car lot
- Rumored affair with Michael Warren
- Interviewed by police, claims ignorance
- Alibi: Solo car repossession (weak, not verified)

**Evidence Found:**
- Witness: Woman matching Sheila's description bought clown makeup/wig at party store days before murder
- Fibers from costume matched material in Sheila's car
- Phone records showed calls between Sheila and Michael
- Balloon/flower purchase traced to local shop

**Problem:** No DNA technology advanced enough, no confession, case stalls

**1995:** Case officially goes cold

### **1990-2002: SUSPICIOUS BEHAVIOR**

**Post-murder:**
- Michael Warren sells used car business
- Moves family to Virginia (suspicious timing)
- Sheila Keen mysteriously moves to Virginia, takes job nearby
- They claim coincidence

**2002:**
- Michael Warren and Sheila Keen get married (12 years after murder)
- Move to Tennessee
- Live quiet life in Abingdon, Virginia area

### **2014-2017: COLD CASE REOPENED**

**2014:**
- Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office Cold Case Unit reopens file
- New DNA technology available
- Re-interview witnesses
- Re-test evidence

**2016:**
- Breakthrough: DNA extracted from orange wig
- Partial genetic profile created
- Familial DNA match → points to Sheila Keen Warren

**September 27, 2017:**
- Grand jury indicts Sheila Keen Warren for first-degree murder
- Arrested in Abingdon, Virginia (age 54)
- Extradition battle begins

**October 2017:**
- Sheila extradited to Florida
- Held in Palm Beach County jail awaiting trial
- Charged: First-degree murder (life without parole potential)

### **2017-2023: PRETRIAL DELAYS**

**Delays due to:**
- COVID-19 pandemic court closures
- Sheila's health issues (brain tumor diagnosis)
- Defense attorney Joe Hurley fights: "junk science" DNA claims
- Evidence disputes
- Legal motions

**7 years in pretrial detention**

### **2023: ALFORD PLEA**

**April 25, 2023:**
- Sheila Keen Warren enters Alford plea to second-degree murder
- Alford plea = acknowledges evidence strong enough for conviction BUT maintains innocence
- Sentence: 20 years
- Credit: 7 years pretrial + Florida gain-time laws
- Effective: ~12 years total, minus time served
- Joseph Airey (victim's son) agrees to plea deal
- Judge accepts plea

**Quote from prosecutor Reid Scott:**
"If this case were to proceed to jury trial, the state would present evidence that on or about May the 26th of 1990, the defendant was responsible for the death of Marlene Warren."

**Defense attorney Greg Rosenfeld:**
"We will never know who killed Marlene Warren because the Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office and the State Attorney's Office did such a poor job investigating this case. I can tell you without question that it was not Sheila Keen."

### **2024: CONTROVERSIAL RELEASE**

**November 2, 2024:**
- Sheila Keen Warren released from Lowell Correctional Institution
- Age: 61
- Time served: 7 years pretrial + 18 months post-conviction = ~8.5 years total
- Reason: Florida gain-time laws (good behavior + education credits)
- Public outrage: "Only 18 months for murder?"

**Quote from attorney Michael Rosenfeld:**
"This is vindication. She did not commit this crime."

**Joseph Airey (victim's son):**
"It was justice, but not the closure we craved."

## 🎭 PSYCHOLOGY & MOTIVE

### **The Affair Motive**

**Sheila + Michael Relationship:**
- Met at car lot (she was employee)
- Affair began: Timeline unclear, but pre-1990
- "Open secret" among coworkers
- Both denied when questioned in 1990

**Why Kill Marlene:**
- Remove obstacle to relationship
- Financial: Michael's assets, business
- Obsession: Sheila wanted Michael exclusively

**The Planning:**
- Sophisticated: Clown disguise hides identity
- Timing: Memorial Day weekend (fewer witnesses around)
- Stolen car: Untraceable
- Escape route: Planned in advance

### **Why a Clown?**

**Psychological Analysis:**
- Disguise: Complete face concealment (makeup + wig)
- Disarming: People don't fear clowns initially (Marlene smiled)
- Misdirection: Absurdity makes witnesses question what they saw
- Memorable but unidentifiable: Everyone remembers clown, nobody can ID person

**Experts suggest:**
- Calculated to exploit human psychology
- Clowns = harmless association
- By time victim realizes danger, too late

### **Living 27 Years Without Confession**

**The Patience:**
- Sheila lived normal life 1990-2017
- Married Michael publicly (2002)
- Operated horse farm
- No slips, no confessions
- Maintained innocence even after arrest

**Psychology:**
- Compartmentalization (able to separate crime from daily life)
- Confidence (believed she'd gotten away with it)
- Sociopathy indicators (lack of remorse, bold actions)
- OR: Truly innocent? (she maintains this)

### **The Alford Plea Psychology**

**Why Plead Guilty While Claiming Innocence:**
- Face life in prison if trial goes badly
- Degraded DNA might not hold up
- 7 years already served
- Could be out soon with plea vs decades if convicted
- Pragmatic: Accept deal vs risk everything

**What It Signals:**
- Avoidance of trial (might reveal more?)
- Calculation: Cut losses
- Maintains narrative for public ("I'm innocent")
