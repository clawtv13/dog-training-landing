CLEVERDOGMETHOD SHORT #11
"Your Dog Already Knows 'Sit' - Stop Repeating It"
Duration: 60-70 seconds (~160 words)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[0:00 - Hook]
[Visual: Owner saying "sit sit sit sit" to confused dog]

Stop saying "sit" five times. Your dog isn't deaf—they're training YOU.

[0:06 - Problem Setup]
[Visual: Multiple owners repeating commands]

Here's what's happening. You say "sit" once. Nothing. Twice. Nothing. Five times—dog finally sits. You reward them.

Congratulations. You just taught your dog to ignore you four times before obeying.

[0:15 - The Science]
[Visual: Simple behavior chain graphic]

Dogs learn patterns. If "sit sit sit sit sit" gets the treat, that's the command they learned. Not "sit."

[0:22 - The Fix]
[Visual: Trainer demonstrating proper technique]

Say it ONCE. Clear and calm. "Sit."

If nothing happens in three seconds, physically guide them into position. The instant their butt touches—YES! Treat.

[0:32 - Why It Works]
[Visual: Side-by-side comparison fast cuts]

Now your dog learns: "Sit" means immediate action. Not negotiation.

[0:37 - Common Mistake]
[Visual: Frustrated owner getting louder]

Don't get louder. Don't repeat. Don't beg.

One command. Three-second window. Guide if needed. Reward instantly.

[0:45 - The Result]
[Visual: Perfect response montage - 3 different dogs, one command, instant sit]

This works with every command. Come. Down. Stay. Heel.

One word. Immediate consequence. That's clarity.

[0:52 - Reality Check]
[Visual: Trainer serious face to camera]

Your dog CAN hear you. They're just waiting for the magic number of repeats that usually works.

Stop training them to ignore you.

[0:59 - CTA]
[Visual: Subscribe animation]

Follow for training that actually works.

[End 1:05]
