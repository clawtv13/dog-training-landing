# 10 Email Automation Prompts for Solo Founders

Stop drowning in email. These 10 ChatGPT prompts handle inbox triaging, response drafting, and meeting prep - the stuff eating 5-10 hours of your week.

---

## 1. Email Triage Agent

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Analyze these emails and categorize each as URGENT (reply today), IMPORTANT (reply this week), or FYI (archive/read later). For URGENT items, explain why in 1 sentence. Format: Email subject | Category | Reason (if urgent)

[Paste your email subjects and 1-line preview here]
```

**When to Use:** Daily, first thing morning or after lunch. Takes 3 minutes to process 40+ emails.

**Time Saved:** 2.5h/week (15 min/day → 3 min/day)

**ROI Calculation:** 2.5 hours × $40/hour = $100/week = $5,200/year

**Example Input:**
```
1. "URGENT: Contract expires Friday" - Legal team
2. "Coffee next week?" - Old college friend  
3. "Invoice #4821 attached" - Vendor
4. "Newsletter: 10 productivity hacks" - Substack
```

**Example Output:**
```
1. URGENT | Contract needs signature by EOD Friday
2. FYI | Social, no business urgency
3. IMPORTANT | Payment due within 30 days
4. FYI | Marketing content, no action needed
```

**Customization Tips:** Add your own categories (DELEGATE, WAITING, SOMEDAY). Include sender name if you prioritize by relationship over content.

**Common Mistakes:** Pasting full email bodies (summaries work better). Not defining what "urgent" means for your business (add examples to prompt).

## 2. Response Template Generator

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Create 5 email response templates for my business. Include: [your top 5 email types]. Each template: subject line, 3-4 sentence body, clear call-to-action. Tone: professional, friendly, concise. Templates should need only 1-2 customized details (name, date, specific ask).
```

**When to Use:** One-time setup, then reuse for 6 months. Update quarterly when your business changes.

**Time Saved:** 3h/week (10 min per response → 2 min)

**ROI Calculation:** 3 hours × $40/hour = $120/week = $6,240/year

**Example Input:**
```
Create templates for: 1) Declining sales calls politely, 2) Requesting payment for overdue invoices, 3) Scheduling discovery calls, 4) Referring inquiries to competitors, 5) Requesting testimonials from happy clients
```

**Example Output:**
```
Template #1 - Decline Sales Call
Subject: Re: [Their pitch topic]
Hi [Name], Thanks for reaching out. We're heads-down on current priorities and not evaluating new tools this quarter. I'll keep you in mind if that changes. Best, [Your name]
```

**Customization Tips:** Include your actual business context ("SaaS founder," "e-commerce," "consulting") for better tone matching. Save templates in your email client as canned responses.

**Common Mistakes:** Making templates too long (3-4 sentences max). Using corporate jargon instead of conversational language.

## 3. Meeting Prep Assistant

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Extract meeting details from this email thread: date/time, attendees, stated purpose. Then create a 3-point prep list: 1) What they want, 2) What I should prepare, 3) Key talking points to drive the conversation.

[Paste email thread here]
```

**When to Use:** Situational - when you have 8+ meetings/week. Run 30 minutes before each call.

**Time Saved:** 2h/week (20 min prep → 5 min)

**ROI Calculation:** 2 hours × $40/hour = $80/week = $4,160/year

**Example Input:**
```
From: Sarah @ Acme Corp
Subject: Partnership discussion - March 15 at 2pm
Hey! Excited to chat about potential collaboration. We're exploring co-marketing opportunities with complementary products. Looking to understand your audience size, typical campaign structure, and what partnerships have worked for you before.
```

**Example Output:**
```
• Date: March 15, 2pm | Attendees: Sarah (Acme) | Purpose: Co-marketing partnership
• PREP: Audience metrics (email list size, engagement rates), 2-3 past partnership case studies, our partnership requirements doc
• TALKING POINTS: Lead with our successful partnership with XYZ Co (+47% leads), ask about their audience overlap, define next steps if fit is good
```

**Customization Tips:** Add your specific prep needs ("competitor research," "pricing authority," "technical feasibility check").

**Common Mistakes:** Prepping for meetings you should decline. Not blocking 5 minutes post-meeting to send follow-up while fresh.

## 4. Follow-Up Automation

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Draft a follow-up email for this scenario: [describe situation]. Tone: [friendly/formal]. Reference our last conversation, add value (not just "checking in"), include specific call-to-action with deadline. Keep under 100 words.

Last contact: [date/summary]
```

**When to Use:** Weekly - batch all follow-ups Friday afternoon. Sends Monday morning.

**Time Saved:** 1.5h/week (15 min each × 6 follow-ups → 3 min each)

**ROI Calculation:** 1.5 hours × $40/hour = $60/week = $3,120/year

**Example Input:**
```
Scenario: Potential client went silent after initial call 2 weeks ago. Last contact: March 1 - they said "need to review budget." Tone: friendly. Want to offer free 30-min consultation to address concerns.
```

**Example Output:**
```
Subject: Quick thought on [their main pain point]

Hi [Name],

Been thinking about the [specific challenge] you mentioned. I'm offering a free 30-min session this week to show exactly how we'd tackle it - no pitch, just actionable ideas you can use either way.

Open Thursday 2-4pm or Friday morning. Worth 30 min?

[Your name]
```

**Customization Tips:** Create templates for different scenarios (cold prospects, warm leads, past clients). Add recent industry news as "value-add" context.

**Common Mistakes:** Generic "checking in" messages (instant delete). Following up too fast (wait 7-10 days minimum).

## 5. Cold Email Personalizer

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
I'm reaching out to [prospect name/company]. Research their recent activity: LinkedIn posts, company news, job postings, tech stack. Write a 2-sentence personalized opener that shows I did homework. No fluff - make it relevant to [your product/service].

Prospect: [name/URL]
What we offer: [1 sentence]
```

**When to Use:** Weekly outreach batches. Spend 20 min researching 10 prospects, then batch prompts.

**Time Saved:** 3.5h/week (30 min per cold email → 5 min)

**ROI Calculation:** 3.5 hours × $40/hour = $140/week = $7,280/year

**Example Input:**
```
Prospect: Jane Chen, Head of Ops @ FastGrow (LinkedIn: fastgrow.com/about)
Recent: Posted about "drowning in manual reporting" - team grew 40% this year
What we offer: Automated dashboard that syncs Stripe, Google Analytics, HubSpot
```

**Example Output:**
```
"Saw your post about reporting chaos with 40% team growth - been there. We built an auto-synced dashboard specifically for ops teams scaling fast, cuts reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes weekly."
```

**Customization Tips:** Use their actual words from posts (builds instant rapport). Link to specific content they created. Add mutual connections if available.

**Common Mistakes:** "Research" that's just company description copy-paste. Compliments without business relevance ("Love your website!").

## 6. Calendar Conflict Resolver

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Someone requested a meeting at [their proposed time]. I'm already booked. Suggest 3 alternative times this week that work better: early morning (8-10am), post-lunch (1-3pm), or late afternoon (4-6pm). Draft a short reply that's accommodating but firm on alternatives.

My availability: [paste calendar blocks]
```

**When to Use:** Situational - when you get 10+ meeting requests/week.

**Time Saved:** 1h/week (6 min per back-and-forth → 2 min)

**ROI Calculation:** 1 hour × $40/hour = $40/week = $2,080/year

**Example Input:**
```
Requested: Wednesday 2pm (I have existing client call)
My availability this week: Tue 9am, Wed 8:30am, Wed 4pm, Thu 1pm, Fri 10am
```

**Example Output:**
```
"I've got a conflict at 2pm Wednesday, but really want to connect. I have openings:
• Tuesday 9am
• Wednesday 8:30am  
• Thursday 1pm

Any of those work? Happy to send a calendar invite once you pick."
```

**Customization Tips:** Add timezone context if working cross-region. Include video link in same message to reduce back-and-forth.

**Common Mistakes:** Offering too many options (3 max). Not checking if meeting is even necessary (suggest async alternative first).

## 7. Invoice Reminder Generator

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Draft a polite payment reminder for invoice [#] issued [date], due [date], amount [$]. Tone: professional, assuming good intent (they're busy, not avoiding). Include: invoice number, amount, due date, payment link, offer to answer questions. Escalate firmness based on: [days overdue].

Days overdue: [number]
```

**When to Use:** Weekly - review receivables every Monday morning. Send reminders for 7, 14, 30 days overdue.

**Time Saved:** 1h/week (12 min per reminder × 5 clients → 3 min each)

**ROI Calculation:** 1 hour × $40/hour = $40/week = $2,080/year

**Example Input:**
```
Invoice #4821 | Issued Feb 28 | Due March 15 | Amount $2,400 | Days overdue: 7
```

**Example Output:**
```
Subject: Invoice #4821 - Quick reminder

Hi [Name],

Hope all's well! Quick reminder that Invoice #4821 ($2,400) came due on March 15. I know things get busy - attaching again for easy reference.

[Payment link]

Any questions on the invoice, just shout. Thanks!

[Your name]
```

**Customization Tips:** Add project context ("Final payment for website redesign"). Scale up firmness at 30+ days ("Please advise on payment timeline").

**Common Mistakes:** Sounding passive-aggressive. Not including payment link (adds friction). Waiting too long to send first reminder.

## 8. Proposal First Draft

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Turn these messy notes into a client proposal. Structure: 1) Problem (their pain), 2) Solution (our approach), 3) Deliverables (specific), 4) Timeline, 5) Investment. Tone: confident but consultative. Keep under 500 words.

Notes: [paste raw meeting notes, ideas, scope discussion]
```

**When to Use:** Weekly - for each new qualified lead. Turn 30-min discovery call into proposal same day.

**Time Saved:** 2.5h/week (90 min drafting → 20 min editing)

**ROI Calculation:** 2.5 hours × $40/hour = $100/week = $5,200/year

**Example Input:**
```
Call with Acme Corp:
- struggling with email marketing, low open rates (12%)
- have 40K list, sending weekly, no segmentation
- want to 2x opens in 90 days
- budget: $3-5K
- need: strategy, templates, setup help
```

**Example Output:**
```
**PROBLEM:** Your 40K list is getting 12% opens (industry avg: 22%) because you're sending one-size-fits-all emails.

**SOLUTION:** 90-day email optimization: audience segmentation (4 groups), A/B tested subject lines, re-engagement campaign for inactive subscribers.

**DELIVERABLES:** Segmentation strategy, 12 email templates, ESP setup, weekly performance review.

**TIMELINE:** Weeks 1-2: Audit & setup | Weeks 3-8: Implementation & testing | Weeks 9-12: Optimization

**INVESTMENT:** $4,200 (payment plan available)
```

**Customization Tips:** Include your "why us" differentiator. Add social proof (relevant case study). Attach pricing options (good/better/best).

**Common Mistakes:** Too long (clients skim). Talking about "us" instead of "them." Vague deliverables ("consulting services").

## 9. Email Summarizer

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Summarize these [number] emails into 5 bullet points. Focus on: action items, decisions needed, important updates. Ignore: pleasantries, signatures, legal disclaimers. Format: • [Sender]: [Key point]

[Paste email content here]
```

**When to Use:** Daily - end of day review. Or after meetings when you have 20+ unread.

**Time Saved:** 2h/week (30 min reading → 5 min)

**ROI Calculation:** 2 hours × $40/hour = $80/week = $4,160/year

**Example Input:**
```
[23 emails from various threads about project timeline, budget approval, vendor questions, team scheduling, and two newsletters]
```

**Example Output:**
```
• Finance: Budget approved for Q2 campaign ($15K), need signed contract by Friday
• Dev Team: Site launch delayed to April 10 (API integration issues)
• Sarah: Wants to reschedule Monday 1:1 to Tuesday
• Vendor: Requesting feedback on logo concepts by EOW
• Legal: NDA signed, can proceed with partnership discussion
```

**Customization Tips:** Add priority flags (🔴 urgent, 🟡 this week, ⚪ FYI). Group by project instead of sender.

**Common Mistakes:** Summarizing each email individually (defeats purpose). Including too much detail (aim for 10 words per bullet).

## 10. Reply Priority Scorer

**Copy-Paste Prompt:**
```
Score these emails 1-10 for reply priority (10 = must respond today). Criteria: revenue impact, relationship value, time-sensitivity, complexity. Show: score, reason (5 words max), suggested response time.

[Paste email subjects + first line]
```

**When to Use:** Daily - when inbox has 30+ emails and you have limited time.

**Time Saved:** 1.5h/week (prevents low-priority rabbit holes)

**ROI Calculation:** 1.5 hours × $40/hour = $60/week = $3,120/year

**Example Input:**
```
1. "Contract ready for signature" - Top client
2. "Quick question on your pricing" - New lead
3. "Speaking opportunity at SmallConf" - Event organizer
4. "Zoom recording from yesterday" - Team member
5. "Your subscription expires soon" - SaaS tool
```

**Example Output:**
```
1. Score: 10 | Top client revenue | Reply: Within 1 hour
2. Score: 8 | Potential new deal | Reply: Today
3. Score: 5 | Nice-to-have exposure | Reply: This week
4. Score: 3 | Internal/no urgency | Reply: When convenient
5. Score: 2 | Auto-renewal spam | Reply: Never (archive)
```

**Customization Tips:** Define your own scoring criteria (add "learning opportunity," "strategic relationship"). Set rules (always prioritize existing clients over cold outreach).

**Common Mistakes:** Treating all "urgent" subject lines as actually urgent. Scoring by sender title instead of actual impact.

## Implementation Tips

**Start with 3 prompts:** Pick the ones that address your biggest time sinks (usually #1, #4, #9).

**Batch processing:** Don't use prompts one-off. Collect 10 emails, process together.

**Save your favorites:** Store customized prompts in your notes app with keyboard shortcuts.

**Measure results:** Track time spent on email for 1 week before, 1 week after. Most users save 4-6 hours/week.

**ROI if you use all 10:** 21h/week saved × $40/hour = $840/week = $43,680/year

Reality check: You'll use 4-6 regularly, save 5-8 hours/week. That's $200-320/week in reclaimed time to build your business instead of managing your inbox.
