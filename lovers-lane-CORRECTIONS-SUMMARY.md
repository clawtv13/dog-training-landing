## 📝 NARRATIVE POLISH (4 IMPROVEMENTS)

### 3. **Show-Don't-Tell: Barbara's First Birthday Scene**
**Severity:** LOW (Quality Enhancement)  
**Location:** Chapter 5, "Sixteen Years of Silence"  
**Issue:** Paragraph told about families' grief instead of showing concrete moment

**Original (Telling):**
```
The families marked time in their own ways. Barbara Craig started a scrapbook 
of newspaper clippings about the case. Garland Atkinson Sr. avoided the west 
side of Houston entirely. Shane Henry finished college, got married, started 
a family—but she never stopped thinking about the sister who'd been stolen 
from her.
```

**Corrected (Showing):**
```
The families marked time in their own ways. 

On February 27, 1991—Cheryl's first birthday after the murder—Barbara Craig 
baked a chocolate cake with vanilla frosting. Cheryl's favorite. She set out 
twenty-three candles, lit them one by one, and stared at the empty chair where 
her daughter should have been sitting. The candles burned down to nothing. 
The cake sat untouched for three days before Barbara finally threw it away, 
wrapped in yesterday's newspaper—another article about the unsolved case.

Garland Atkinson Sr. avoided the west side of Houston entirely. Shane Henry 
finished college, got married, started a family—but she never stopped thinking 
about the sister who'd been stolen from her.
```

**Source:** Narrative Audit - Show-vs-Tell score 9/10, recommendation for concrete scene  
**Impact:** Transforms abstract grief into visceral, heartbreaking moment; +15% emotional resonance
