# Calmora Ad Creative Strategy — Back Stretcher (TikTok + Meta)

**Product:** Calmora Spinal Stretcher / Back Pain Relief Device
**Budget:** $600–1000 initial allocation
**Primary platforms:** TikTok (validation), Meta (scale)
**Target audience:** Adults 30–55 with back pain, desk workers, active lifestyle seekers

---

## Ad Strategy Overview

### Primary Positioning
**"3 minutes a day. $40 one-time. Relief that lasts."**

- **What it is:** At-home spinal decompression device with 88 acupressure points
- **What it does:** Stretches spine, relieves tension, targets muscle knots
- **For whom:** Anyone with back pain, desk workers, post-workout recovery
- **Why now:** WFH back pain epidemic, rising chiro costs, DIY wellness trend
- **Why Calmora:** Fraction of chiro cost ($40 vs $960/year), no appointments, use forever

### Pain Points to Target
1. **Cost frustration** - "I can't afford $80/week for chiro visits"
2. **Time scarcity** - "I don't have time for appointments"
3. **Chronic discomfort** - "My back hurts after sitting all day"
4. **Skepticism of pills** - "I don't want to rely on medication"
5. **Failed solutions** - "I've tried stretching but nothing works"

### Key Differentiators
- 3 adjustable arch levels (gentle → intense)
- 88 acupressure points for muscle release
- No batteries/setup - just lie back
- Supports up to 330 lbs
- 30-day money-back guarantee

## TikTok Creative Angles (Phase 1: Validation)

### Angle 1: The Math Hook
**Hook:** "I was spending $320/month on chiropractor visits..."
- **Format:** 15-20 second talking head
- **Scene:** Person at desk, then using stretcher on floor
- **Script:**
  - "I was spending $320/month on the chiropractor"
  - "Then I found this $40 thing on TikTok"
  - "3 minutes a day. Same relief. Forever."
  - [shows using it] "My back hasn't felt this good in years"
- **Text overlay:** "$320/mo → $40 once
- **CTA:** "Link in bio"
- **Why it works:** Money-saving hooks crush on TikTok. Relatable pain point.

### Angle 2: Desk Worker Transformation
**Hook:** "POV: You work from home and your back is screaming"
- **Format:** 15 seconds split screen
- **Scene:** 
  - Before: Hunched at desk, rubbing lower back, grimacing
  - After: Lying on stretcher with relief face, then standing tall
- **Audio:** Trending transformation sound
- **Text:** "8 hours of sitting → 3 minutes of this"
- **CTA:** "Get relief → link"
- **Why it works:** WFH audience is massive. Visual transformation sells.

### Angle 3: ASMR/Satisfying Cracking
**Hook:** [No text, just sound of back cracking/stretching]
- **Format:** 15 seconds pure visual
- **Scene:** Close-up of person lying on device, gentle arch adjustment, satisfying stretch sounds
- **Audio:** Natural sounds (no music) or soft ASMR
- **Text overlay:** "That first stretch hits different"
- **CTA:** Soft end card
- **Why it works:** ASMR/satisfying content = high engagement. Sound ON hooks.

### Angle 4: Morning Routine Integration
**Hook:** "My non-negotiable morning routine for back pain"
- **Format:** 20 second routine video
- **Scene:** 
  - Wake up
  - Coffee brewing
  - 3 minutes on stretcher
  - Standing up refreshed
- **Audio:** Lo-fi morning vibe
- **Text:** "Coffee + 3 min stretch = no pain day"
- **CTA:** "This changed my mornings"
- **Why it works:** Routine content performs well. Shows ease of use.

### Angle 5: Skeptic Converted
**Hook:** "I thought these TikTok back things were BS..."
- **Format:** 20 second testimonial style
- **Script:**
  - "Okay I'll admit it, I thought this was another TikTok gimmick"
  - "But my back was killing me so I tried it"
  - [using device] "Day 1 I felt something"
  - "Week 2, I haven't touched my heating pad"
  - "It's been 3 months. Still using it every day."
- **Text:** "Skeptic → believer"
- **CTA:** "Don't knock it till you try it"
- **Why it works:** Overcomes objections. Authentic skeptic angle builds trust.
