# Why I Switched From VAs to OpenClaw

I was burning $2,400/month on virtual assistants. Two part-timers at $15/hour, 20 hours each per week.

The real cost? Way higher.

Every task needed a briefing. A Loom video here, a Slack thread there. Training took weeks. Then they'd quit or ghost, and I'd start over. One VA took 3 days to learn my content workflow. The replacement took 5.

I spent 8-10 hours per week managing them. That's another $800 in my time if I valued it at $100/hour (I do).

Total monthly burn: $3,200+

OpenClaw runs 24/7 for $47/month. It doesn't need Loom videos. It doesn't quit. It executes the same task the same way every time. No Slack check-ins. No "hey, quick question about this thing I should already know."

I pointed it at my YouTube workflow, my Reddit scraping, my content pipeline. Done. It handles what used to take my VAs 15+ hours in under an hour.

The math is stupid simple: $3,200/month vs $47/month.

I got my weekends back. OpenClaw doesn't need handholding.

If you're paying VAs to do repetitive work, you're paying the wrong labor model.
