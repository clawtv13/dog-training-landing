This isn't about perfection. It's about **progress**. Each small win builds on the last.

## WEEK 1: Foundation & First Days
### Days 1-7: The Critical Setup Period

---

### **Day 1: Homecoming & Safety Setup**

**Goal:** Create a safe space and establish first routines

**What to Do:**

1. **Designate a "puppy zone"**
   - Use baby gates to limit access to 1-2 rooms
   - Remove hazards (cords, small objects, toxic plants)
   - Set up crate with comfy bedding

2. **First bathroom break ritual**
   - Take puppy outside immediately on arrival
   - Use the SAME spot every time
   - Praise calmly when they go

3. **Establish meal times**
   - Feed at same times daily (3-4 meals for young puppies)
   - Remove food after 20 minutes

**Expected Results:** Puppy explores safely, has first successful potty outside

**Troubleshooting:**
- Puppy won't eat? Normal - give it 24 hours
- Cries in crate? Cover it, add warm water bottle
- Accidents inside? Clean with enzyme cleaner, no punishment

☐ Completed Day 1

---

### **Day 2: Name Recognition & Potty Schedule**

**Goal:** Puppy learns their name, begin potty training pattern

**What to Do:**

1. **Name game (5 min, 3x today)**
   - Say puppy's name in happy voice
   - When they look at you → treat + praise
   - Repeat 10 times per session

2. **Hourly potty breaks**
   - Set phone alarm every hour
   - Take to same spot
   - Wait 5 minutes
   - Praise + treat when they go

3. **Track successes**
   - Note what times puppy pees/poops
   - Patterns emerge quickly

**Expected Results:** Puppy responds to name 50% of the time, 2-3 successful outdoor potties

**Troubleshooting:**
- Puppy ignores name? Use higher-value treats
- No potty outside? Walk in circles, be patient
- Accidents increasing? You're not going out often enough

☐ Completed Day 2

---

### **Day 3: Crate Training Basics**

**Goal:** Puppy sees crate as safe den, not prison

**What to Do:**

1. **Make crate irresistible**
   - Toss treats inside throughout day
   - Feed meals in crate (door open)
   - Hide favorite toy inside

2. **First short sessions**
   - Puppy goes in voluntarily → close door 10 seconds
   - Open before they whine
   - Repeat 5 times

3. **Naptime = crate time**
   - Puppies sleep 18-20 hours/day
   - Use crate for all naps
   - Potty break immediately after waking

**Expected Results:** Puppy enters crate willingly for treats, tolerates 30 seconds with door closed

**Troubleshooting:**
- Screaming in crate? Don't let out while crying (teaches that screaming works)
- Won't go in? Make treat trail leading inside
- Poops in crate? Crate may be too large (should only fit standing/turning)

☐ Completed Day 3

---

### **Day 4: Bite Inhibition Begins**

**Goal:** Teach puppy that teeth on skin = playtime stops

**What to Do:**

1. **The "ouch!" method**
   - When puppy bites: Say "OUCH!" (high-pitched)
   - Stop all interaction
   - Turn away for 10 seconds
   - Resume play

2. **Redirect to toys**
   - Keep toy in pocket always
   - When biting starts → offer toy instead
   - Praise when they bite toy

3. **No hands as toys**
   - Never wrestle with hands
   - Use rope toys for tug

**Expected Results:** Puppy pauses briefly when you say "ouch", begins choosing toys over hands 30% of time

**Troubleshooting:**
- Biting gets worse? They're overtired - enforce naptime
- "Ouch" excites them more? Use calmer voice, still turn away
- Bites hard? End ALL play immediately, leave room

☐ Completed Day 4

---

### **Day 5: Socialization - Sounds & Surfaces**

**Goal:** Expose puppy to new experiences safely

**What to Do:**

1. **Sound desensitization**
   - Play YouTube "puppy socialization sounds" at LOW volume
   - Treat while sounds play
   - Gradually increase volume over days

2. **Surface exploration**
   - Walk puppy on: carpet, tile, grass, concrete, gravel
   - Treat on each new surface
   - Keep sessions under 5 minutes

3. **Home visitors (if vaccinated enough)**
   - Invite calm friend over
   - Guest ignores puppy initially
   - Reward puppy for calm behavior

**Expected Results:** Puppy curious about new sounds/surfaces, not fearful

**Troubleshooting:**
- Puppy scared? Back off, slower exposure
- Too excited? Shorter sessions
- Barking at sounds? Lower volume, increase treats

☐ Completed Day 5

---

### **Day 6: Handling & Grooming Prep**

**Goal:** Puppy tolerates being touched everywhere

**What to Do:**

1. **Body handling (3 min sessions, 3x today)**
   - Touch paws → treat
   - Touch ears → treat
   - Touch mouth/teeth → treat
   - Touch tail → treat

2. **Mock grooming**
   - Run brush gently over coat
   - Hold nail clippers near paws (don't cut yet)
   - Look in ears

3. **Restraint practice**
   - Gently hold puppy still for 5 seconds
   - Release + treat
   - Build up to 30 seconds

**Expected Results:** Puppy accepts paw touches without pulling away

**Troubleshooting:**
- Puppy squirms? Shorter holds, better treats
- Bites during handling? Go slower, treat faster
- Fear response? Break it down further (touch = treat, don't hold yet)

☐ Completed Day 6

---

### **Day 7: Review & Assess**

**Goal:** Solidify Week 1 skills, identify gaps

**What to Do:**

1. **Test name recognition**
   - Call puppy from across room
   - Score: How often do they come? ___/10

2. **Potty training check-in**
   - Count accidents this week: ___
   - Successful outside potties: ___
   - Goal: 80% success rate

3. **Crate comfort**
   - Can puppy stay in crate 1 hour during day? Y/N
   - Sleep through night (or wake once)? Y/N

4. **Plan Week 2 focus areas**

**Expected Results:** Clear picture of what's working, what needs more work

**Week 1 Wins:**
- ☐ Puppy knows name
- ☐ Potty pattern established
- ☐ Crate = safe space
- ☐ Biting less intense
- ☐ Basic handling accepted

☐ Completed Day 7 ✅

## WEEK 2: Communication & Impulse Control
### Days 8-14: Building Connection

---

### **Day 8: "Sit" Command Introduction**

**Goal:** Puppy offers sit behavior reliably

**What to Do:**

1. **Luring method**
   - Hold treat at puppy's nose
   - Slowly move up and back
   - Butt hits floor → "YES!" + treat
   - Repeat 10 times

2. **Add verbal cue**
   - Only say "sit" AFTER you see the sit happening
   - Butt drops → "Sit" → treat
   - Do this 20 times today

3. **Practice in different rooms**

**Expected Results:** Puppy sits when you lure 80% of the time

**Troubleshooting:**
- Jumps instead? Hold treat lower
- Backs up? Practice against wall
- Won't sit? They might not be food-motivated - try toys

☐ Completed Day 8

---

### **Day 9: Leave It / Drop It Basics**

**Goal:** Interrupt unwanted behavior

**What to Do:**

1. **"Leave it" game**
   - Show treat in closed fist
   - Puppy will paw/lick hand
   - Wait... they'll give up
   - When they stop trying → open hand + treat
   - Repeat 15 times

2. **Add verbal cue**
   - As they give up → say "Leave it"
   - Mark moment + reward

3. **Real-world practice**
   - Drop toy, say "leave it"
   - When they stop → redirect to different toy

**Expected Results:** Puppy pauses action 50% of time when you say "leave it"

**Troubleshooting:**
- Puppy too intense? Use lower-value item
- Won't give up? Wait longer - patience!
- Grabbing anyway? Keep treat in closed fist

## BONUS SECTION: Top 10 Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Inconsistency** - Different rules from different people confuses puppy
2. **Punishment for accidents** - Creates fear, not understanding
3. **Skipping socialization** - Critical window closes at 16 weeks
4. **Expecting too much too fast** - Puppies are babies, be patient
5. **Not enough exercise** - Tired puppy = good puppy
6. **Free feeding** - Scheduled meals = predictable potty times
7. **Ignoring small bites** - They escalate if not addressed
8. **Overwhelming with visitors** - Quality over quantity
9. **Delaying vet visits** - Early health checks catch issues
10. **Giving up too soon** - Consistency takes time!
