## The Truth About House Training

**Average Timeline:** 4-6 months for full reliability
**Your Goal:** 80% success rate by week 4

House training isn't about teaching your puppy WHERE to go—it's about creating a routine so predictable that accidents become rare.

---

## Before You Start

###What You Need:
- ☐ Enzyme cleaner (Nature's Miracle, Rocco & Roxie)
- ☐ Crate (just big enough to stand/turn)
- ☐ Leash for potty breaks
- ☐ High-value treats (tiny, smelly)
- ☐ This printable tracker

### The 3 Rules That Make or Break Success:

**1. Supervision or Confinement** - Never "freedom" until fully trained
**2. Routine = Everything** - Same times, same place, every single day  
**3. Clean Accidents Properly** - Wrong cleaner = puppy returns to same spot

## Your Puppy's Potty Schedule

**How Often Puppies Need To Go:**
- **8-10 weeks:** Every 1-2 hours
- **10-12 weeks:** Every 2-3 hours
- **12-16 weeks:** Every 3-4 hours
- **16-20 weeks:** Every 4-5 hours
- **20+ weeks:** Can hold 6-8 hours (daytime only)

**ALWAYS take puppy out:**
- ☐ First thing in morning
- ☐ After every meal (within 15 min)
- ☐ After waking from nap
- ☐ After play session
- ☐ Before bed
- ☐ Middle of night if under 12 weeks

## Week 1: Foundation Training

### Day 1-3: Establish The Spot

**Morning Routine (repeat all day):**
1. Carry puppy outside immediately
2. Go to THE SAME SPOT every time
3. Stand still, boring, no talking
4. Wait 5 minutes
5. When puppy goes → "Yes! Good potty!" + treat immediately
6. Play for 5 minutes as reward
7. Return inside

**Why The Same Spot:**
- Puppy's own scent triggers them to go
- Builds habit faster
- You'll know if they went

**Throughout The Day:**
- Set phone alarm every 2 hours
- Supervise 100% when awake
- Watch for sniffing, circling, whining

**Crate Time:**
- Puppies won't soil their bed (usually)
- Use crate for all unsupervised time
- Crate = management tool, not punishment

### Day 4-7: Pattern Recognition

**You're Looking For:**
- What times does puppy pee most?
- How long after eating do they poop?
- Any "tells" before going? (sniffing, circling)

**Track Everything:**
- Use tracker at end of this guide
- Note patterns
- Adjust schedule accordingly

**Success This Week:**
☐ 10+ successful outdoor potties
☐ Fewer than 5 indoor accidents
☐ Puppy starting to signal (go to door, whine)

## Week 2-4: Building Reliability

### Increase Time Between Breaks

**Gradually extend** (only if zero accidents):
- Week 2: Every 2.5 hours
- Week 3: Every 3 hours
- Week 4: Every 3.5 hours

**The Golden Rule:** If accidents increase, you extended too fast. Go back one step.

### Teach "Go Potty" Command

**Why This Matters:** Eventually you can trigger potty on command (huge for travel!)

**How To Train:**
1. Just as puppy starts to squat
2. Say "go potty" or "hurry up"
3. When they finish → Treat + praise
4. Repeat 50+ times
5. Eventually say command BEFORE they go
6. Works in 2-3 weeks

### Handle Accidents Correctly

**When You Catch Them:**
- Interrupt with "Ah-ah!" (not angry, just attention-getting)
- Scoop up puppy mid-stream if possible
- Rush outside to finish
- Praise if they finish outside
- Clean spot with enzyme cleaner

**When You Find Old Accident:**
- Do NOTHING to puppy (they have zero memory)
- Clean thoroughly
- Question: How did puppy get unsupervised access?
- Fix management failure

**Never:**
- ☐ Rub nose in it (teaches fear, not housetraining)
- ☐ Yell or hit (damages trust)
- ☐ React emotionally to accidents

## Common Problems & Solutions

### Problem: Accidents Always In Same Spot

**Why:** Scent remains, even if you can't smell it
**Solution:**
- Use enzyme cleaner (not regular cleaner!)
- Soak area, let sit 10 min, blot
- Block access to that room for 2 weeks
- Consider black light to find all spots

### Problem: Goes Outside, Then Immediately Inside

**Why:** Outside is too exciting to focus
**Solution:**
- Boring potty breaks (no play first)
- Stand still in one spot
- Wait full 5 minutes
- Play ONLY after successful potty
- Keep on leash

### Problem: Holds It Forever Outside

**Why:** Distracted, not in routine spot, scared of weather
**Solution:**
- Morning: Don't feed breakfast until after poop
- Walk in circles (movement helps)
- Try different spot in yard
- Use umbrella if raining
- Be patient—wait 10 minutes

### Problem: Pees In Crate

**Causes & Solutions:**
- **Crate too big:** Divide with cardboard
- **Puppy from pet store:** Never learned not to soil bed—will take 2x longer
- **Medical issue:** Vet check (UTI common)
- **Too long crated:** Reduce time, add mid-day break

### Problem: Rings Bell But Wants To Play

**Solution:**
- Boring potty breaks only
- If no potty in 5 min → Back to crate for 15 min
- Try again
- Eventually learns: Only pee = freedom

## Nighttime House Training

### Under 12 Weeks:

**Expect:** 1-2 nighttime potty breaks
**How To Handle:**
- Set alarm before puppy wakes you
- Carry straight outside
- No play, no talking
- Back to crate immediately after

### 12-16 Weeks:

**Goal:** Sleep through night
**How:**
- No water 2 hours before bed
- Late-night potty break (11pm+)
- If puppy wakes: Take out quickly, return to crate
- Don't play or puppy learns waking = fun

### 16+ Weeks:

Should sleep 7-8 hours no problem
If not → Medical check needed

## Bell Training (Optional But Helpful)

**Setup:**
1. Hang bells on door handle (low enough for puppy)
2. Before every outside trip → Guide puppy's paw to ring bells
3. Immediately open door
4. After 1 week, wait for puppy to ring themselves
5. Jackpot treats when they do!

**Works in:** 1-2 weeks
**Bonus:** Clear communication = fewer accidents

## When To Worry

**See a vet if:**
- ☐ Frequent urination (every 30 min)
- ☐ Straining to pee
- ☐ Blood in urine
- ☐ Suddenly regresses after being trained
- ☐ Drinking excessively
- ☐ Peeing large amounts in crate
- ☐ Over 8 months old, still not improving

**House training is a MANAGEMENT problem, not a dog problem.**

This isn't to make you feel guilty—it's to empower you. YOU control the variables:
- Frequency of breaks
- Level of supervision
- Routine consistency
- Proper cleaning

**Get these right = Success guaranteed.**
