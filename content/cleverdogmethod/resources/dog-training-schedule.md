## The 15-Minute Rule

**Science-backed truth:** Dogs learn better in short, frequent sessions than long ones.

**3 sessions of 15 minutes > 1 session of 45 minutes**

Why?
- Maintains focus and motivation
- Prevents frustration
- Ends on success
- Builds consistency

## Your Training Week Template

### Monday: Foundation Commands

**Morning Session (15 min)**
- 5 min: Sit (10 reps)
- 5 min: Down (10 reps)
- 5 min: Stay (increase duration)

**Evening Session (15 min)**
- Review all 3 commands
- Add distractions (toy nearby)
- End with play reward

**Success Metrics:**
- Sit: ☐ 90%+ first-time compliance
- Down: ☐ 80%+ first-time compliance
- Stay: ☐ Hold for __ seconds

---

### Tuesday: Recall Training

**Morning Session (15 min)**
- Indoors, no distractions
- Call "come" from 5 feet → Treat + praise
- Gradually increase distance
- 20 repetitions

**Evening Session (15 min)**
- Outdoors (fenced + long-line for safety)
- Call from 10 feet
- Jackpot treats (handful)
- 15 repetitions

**Never:** Call your dog to something unpleasant

**Success Check:**
☐ Comes immediately when called
☐ No hesitation
☐ Reliable at __ feet distance

---

### Wednesday: Leash Manners

**Morning Session (15 min)**
- Practice inside first
- Reward position at your side
- Stop when pulling, move when loose
- 3 minutes walking, 2 minutes rest (repeat 3x)

**Evening Session (15 min)**
- Short walk outside
- High-value treats for loose leash
- Change direction when pulling
- 10-minute walk max (quality > distance)

**Progress Tracker:**
- Pulling frequency: ☐ Constant ☐ Often ☐ Sometimes ☐ Rare
- Attention on you: ☐ Never ☐ Sometimes ☐ Frequently ☐ Always

---

### Thursday: Impulse Control

**Morning Session (15 min)**
- "Wait" before meals (start 3 seconds, build to 30)
- "Leave it" with treats on ground
- "Stay" with temptation (toy visible)

**Evening Session (15 min)**
- "Wait" at doorways
- "Drop it" trade game
- Door manners (sit before going out)

**Impulse Control = Problem Prevention**

These exercises prevent:
- Door bolting
- Counter surfing
- Resource guarding
- Leash reactivity

---

### Friday: Socialization & Exposure

**Morning Session (15 min)**
- New surfaces (bubble wrap, cardboard, etc.)
- Novel objects (umbrella, vacuum, etc.)
- Treat for calm investigation

**Evening Session (15 min)**
- Car ride practice
- Visit pet-friendly store
- OR walk in new neighborhood
- Focus: Positive exposure, not training commands

**Socialization Checklist:**
☐ New person
☐ New dog (if safe/vaccinated)
☐ New place
☐ New sound
☐ New surface

---

### Saturday: Fun Training Games

**Morning Session (15 min)**
- Hide and seek
- Treasure hunt (hide treats)
- Trick training (shake, spin, play dead)

**Evening Session (15 min)**
- "Find it" scent game
- Tug (with rules - release on cue)
- Free play reward

**Why Games Matter:**
- Build relationship
- Mental exercise
- Reinforce commands in fun context
- Keep dog motivated

---

### Sunday: REST DAY

**No formal training sessions**

Instead:
- Review commands casually throughout day
- Reward good behavior as it happens
- Long walk or play date
- Mental enrichment (puzzle toys, snuffle mat)

**Rest days prevent:**
- Burnout (yours and dog's)
- Training plateau
- Frustration

| Command | Goal |

| Command | Goal |

| Command | Goal |
|---------|------|
| Sit | At vet, pet store, park |

## Training Tips For Success

### Before Each Session:
1. **Have treats ready** (tiny, high-value)
2. **Eliminate distractions** (TV off, kids quiet)
3. **Set timer** (stop at 15 min even if going well)
4. **Have clear goal** (what specific skill today?)

### During Session:
- **80% success minimum** - If below, make it easier
- **End on success** - Last rep should be easy win
- **Use marker word** - "Yes!" right when they do it right
- **Vary rewards** - Treats, play, praise

### After Session:
- **Note progress** - Track what worked
- **Reward with play** - 5 min free play
- **Don't repeat later** - Let learning consolidate

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

**"My dog isn't motivated by treats"**
- Try higher-value (chicken, cheese, hot dog)
- Train before meals when hungry
- Use toys if truly food-disinterested

**"Sessions always go longer than 15 minutes"**
- Set phone alarm
- Quality > quantity
- Tired dog learns nothing

**"I skip days when busy"**
- 5 minutes is better than zero
- Training can be during meals, walks
- Consistency matters more than length

**"My dog regresses between sessions"**
- Normal! Learning isn't linear
- May need more repetitions at easier level
- Proof commands in different locations

## Advanced: Customizing Your Schedule

**For Puppies (8-16 weeks):**
- Keep all sessions to 5 minutes
- Focus on basics only
- More socialization, less command drilling
- 3-4 mini-sessions per day

**For Adolescent Dogs (6-18 months):**
- Expect regression—be patient
- More impulse control work
- Increase distractions gradually
- May need 4+ sessions/day during "teenage" phase

**For Adult Dogs:**
- Can handle 20-minute sessions
- Faster learning curve
- May have bad habits to unlearn
- Still need consistency

**For Reactive/Fearful Dogs:**
- Shorter sessions (10 min)
- More focus on calm behavior
- Positive reinforcement only
- May need professional help
