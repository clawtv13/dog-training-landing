## LEVEL 1: BEGINNER (All dogs can do these)

### Game 1: The Cup Game
**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Builds:** Focus, problem-solving

**What You Need:** 3 cups, treats

**How To Play:**
1. Show dog treat
2. Place under one cup
3. Shuffle cups slowly
4. Let dog investigate
5. When they paw/nose correct cup → Lift cup + praise
6. Repeat 10 times
7. Gradually shuffle faster

**Make It Harder:** Add more cups, faster shuffling

---

### Game 2: Which Hand?
**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆
**Time:** 3 minutes
**Builds:** Scent work, focus

**What You Need:** Treats, your hands

**How To Play:**
1. Put treat in one closed fist
2. Present both fists to dog
3. Dog will sniff/paw
4. When they choose correct hand → Open + treat
5. Wrong choice → Show correct hand, no treat
6. Repeat 15 times

**Pro Tip:** Don't help by leaning toward correct hand

---

### Game 3: Find The Treats
**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Builds:** Scent discrimination, hunting instinct

**What You Need:** 10-15 small treats

**How To Play:**
1. Put dog in "stay" or have helper hold them
2. Hide treats around room (easy spots first)
3. Release with "find it!"
4. Watch them hunt
5. Praise each discovery

**Progression:**
- Week 1: Treats visible
- Week 2: Treats partially hidden
- Week 3: Treats fully hidden
- Week 4: Hide in different room

---

## LEVEL 2: INTERMEDIATE

### Game 4: Treasure Hunt With Toys
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
**Time:** 10-15 minutes
**Builds:** Scent tracking, retrieval

**What You Need:** Favorite toy

**How To Play:**
1. Show dog toy
2. Tell them "stay" or put in other room
3. Hide toy (start easy)
4. Release: "Find [toy name]!"
5. Huge praise when found
6. Repeat 5 times, harder hiding spots

**Advanced:** Hide multiple toys, dog must find all

---

### Game 5: The Name Game
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Builds:** Object discrimination, language skills

**What You Need:** 2-3 different toys

**How To Play:**
1. Name each toy consistently ("ball", "rope", "squeaky")
2. Lay toys on floor
3. Ask for specific toy: "Get ball"
4. If correct → Jackpot treats
5. If wrong → No reaction, try again
6. Build up to 5-10 named toys

**Dogs Can Learn:** 100+ object names

---

### Game 6: Muffin Tin Puzzle
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
**Time:** 5-10 minutes
**Builds:** Problem-solving, scent work

**What You Need:** Muffin tin, treats, tennis balls

**How To Play:**
1. Place treats in some muffin cups
2. Cover all cups with tennis balls
3. Let dog figure out how to remove balls
4. Praise each success

**Make It Harder:**
- Treats in fewer cups
- Use different sized balls
- Stack balls

---

## LEVEL 3: ADVANCED

### Game 7: The 3-Bucket Challenge
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Builds:** Memory, impulse control

**What You Need:** 3 buckets/cups, treats

**How To Play:**
1. Show dog which bucket has treat
2. Cover all buckets
3. Make dog "sit" and "wait" for 5 seconds
4. Release to find treat
5. Gradually increase wait time
6. Add shuffling buckets before wait

**Tests:** Working memory and self-control

---

### Game 8: Hide and Seek (You!)
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Builds:** Recall, scent tracking, bond

**What You Need:** Nothing!

**How To Play:**
1. Have helper hold dog (or use "stay")
2. Hide somewhere in house
3. Call "come!" or dog's name
4. When dog finds you → JACKPOT party
5. Repeat 5-10 times
6. Hide in harder spots each time

**Kid-Friendly:** Great game for children to play with dog

---

### Game 9: The Box Game
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
**Time:** 10-15 minutes
**Builds:** Problem-solving, creativity

**What You Need:** Cardboard boxes (various sizes), treats

**How To Play:**
1. Place treats in/under/around boxes
2. Let dog investigate
3. Reward: Pawing, nosing, moving boxes
4. Don't help—let them problem-solve
5. Destroy the boxes? That's okay! (Supervised only)

**Make It Harder:**
- Boxes inside boxes
- Tape some closed
- Stack boxes

---

### Game 10: DIY Snuffle Mat
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
**Time:** 20 minutes (play)
**Builds:** Scent work, foraging instinct, calm behavior

**What You Need:** Towel or fleece strips, treats

**How To Play:**
1. Scrunch up towel
2. Hide treats in all folds
3. Let dog use nose to find
4. Praise calm, focused searching

**OR Make Real Snuffle Mat:**
- Rubber sink mat + fleece strips tied through holes
- Lasts forever, washable

**Benefits:**
- Tires dog mentally
- Slows down fast eaters
- Calming activity

## Game Selection Guide

**For Puppies (8-16 weeks):**
- Cup Game
- Which Hand?
- Find The Treats
→ Keep it super simple, 5-min sessions max

**For High-Energy Dogs:**
- Treasure Hunt
- Hide and Seek
- The Box Game
→ Maximum mental exhaustion

**For Seniors:**
- Which Hand?
- Muffin Tin Puzzle
- Snuffle Mat
→ Low physical demand, high mental

**For Reactive Dogs:**
- Name Game
- The Box Game
- Snuffle Mat
→ Builds focus, reduces frustration

---

## How To Structure Game Time

**Daily Schedule:**

**Morning (before work):**
- 1 quick game (5 min)
- Tires dog, prevents destructive behavior while alone

**Afternoon:**
- 1 medium game (10 min)
- Keeps dog engaged

**Evening:**
- 1-2 games (15 min total)
- Winding down energy

**Before Bed:**
- Snuffle mat or calm game
- Promotes sleep

**Total:** 30-40 minutes/day = tired, happy dog

---

## Rules For Successful Brain Game Sessions

### DO:
✅ Keep sessions short (end before boredom)
✅ Let dog problem-solve (don't help immediately)
✅ Celebrate every success
✅ Rotate games (variety prevents boredom)
✅ End on success

### DON'T:
❌ Force participation (if dog not interested, try different game)
❌ Make it too hard too fast
❌ Punish "wrong" choices
❌ Skip physical exercise (need both!)

## Bonus: Free DIY Puzzle Toys

### Toilet Paper Roll Puzzle
1. Fold ends of empty roll
2. Put treats inside
3. Let dog destroy to get treats
(Supervise—don't let them eat cardboard)

### Frozen Kong
1. Stuff Kong with peanut butter + kibble
2. Freeze overnight
3. Give to dog
4. Lasts 30-60 minutes

### Towel Burrito
1. Lay treats on towel
2. Roll up tightly
3. Tie ends loosely
4. Dog must unroll to find treats
