## Understanding Puppy Biting

Puppy biting is **completely normal**—but that doesn't mean you have to tolerate those needle-sharp teeth! This 7-day plan will teach your puppy that:
- Human skin is off-limits
- Toys are the appropriate outlet for chewing
- Gentle mouth = good things happen

**Success Rate:** 85% of puppies show significant improvement within one week when owners follow this protocol consistently.

---

## Before You Start

### What You'll Need:
- ☐ 5-10 different chew toys (variety is key)
- ☐ Soft puppy treats
- ☐ Timeout area (playpen or small room)
- ☐ This tracking sheet (print it!)

### Important Rules:
1. **Everyone must be consistent** - If one person allows biting, training fails
2. **Never hit or yell** - This teaches fear, not bite inhibition
3. **Patience required** - Puppies learn through repetition (20-50+ times)

---

## DAY 1: The Foundation

### Goal: Interrupt All Biting

**Morning Session (10 minutes):**
1. Play with puppy using toy ONLY
2. When teeth touch skin → High-pitched "OUCH!"
3. Immediately stop play, stand up, turn away
4. Count to 10 in silence
5. Resume play with toy
6. Repeat 10 times

**Throughout The Day:**
- Every single bite gets the same response
- No exceptions, no "soft bites allowed"
- Track successes: How many times did puppy stop after "ouch"? _____

**Evening Session (10 minutes):**
- Repeat morning protocol
- Add: Praise calmly when puppy licks instead of bites
- Treat when they choose toy over your hand

### Day 1 Success Indicators:
☐ Puppy paused at least once after "ouch"
☐ You stayed consistent all day
☐ All family members on board

**Troubleshooting:**
- **"Ouch makes them bite harder!"** → Use calmer tone, but still turn away
- **"They think it's a game"** → Timeout (see Day 3) if turning away isn't enough
- **"They won't stop!"** → They're overtired. Puppies need 18-20 hours sleep daily.

---

## DAY 2: Redirect to Toys

### Goal: Teach "Bite This, Not That"

**New Technique: Active Redirection**

**Morning Session:**
1. Keep toy in pocket/hand always
2. When puppy approaches to bite → Offer toy BEFORE teeth touch skin
3. When they bite toy → "Yes! Good bite!" + treat
4. Repeat 15 times

**Throughout The Day:**
- Never let puppy succeed at biting skin
- Always have toy ready
- Reward toy-biting heavily

**Evening Session:**
- Play tug with appropriate toy
- Let puppy win sometimes (builds confidence)
- When they release toy on command ("Drop it") → Jackpot treats

### Day 2 Success Indicators:
☐ Puppy chose toy over hand at least 3 times
☐ Reduced biting attempts
☐ Longer play sessions without biting

**What's Normal:**
- Puppy still tries to bite you (they're learning!)
- Some days seem worse (growth spurts = more mouthy)
- Teething peaks around 3-6 months

---

## DAY 3: Timeout Consequences

### Goal: Biting = Fun Stops

**Introduce Timeout Protocol**

**When To Use:**
- Puppy bites hard after "ouch"
- Biting escalates during play
- Repeated biting within 2 minutes

**How To Do Timeout:**
1. Calmly say "Too bad"
2. Pick up puppy (or leave room yourself)
3. Place in timeout area (playpen/bathroom)
4. Close door/gate
5. Wait 30-60 seconds
6. Return without emotion
7. Resume play only if puppy is calm

**Important:** Timeout is NOT punishment. It's removing access to fun.

### Day 3 Tracking:
- Timeouts given today: _____
- Did biting reduce after timeout? ☐ Yes ☐ No
- Puppy calm when released? ☐ Yes ☐ No

---

## DAY 4: Impulse Control Games

### Goal: Teach Self-Control

**Brain Over Teeth**

**Game 1: "Wait for It"**
1. Hold treat in closed fist
2. Puppy will paw/mouth hand
3. Wait in silence
4. When puppy backs off → Open hand + treat
5. Repeat 10 times
6. Add verbal cue "gentle" or "easy"

**Game 2: "Sit for Greeting"**
- Before petting → Ask for sit
- Only pet when sitting
- Stand up if jumping/biting occurs
- Teaches: Calm = attention

**Game 3: "Tug with Rules"**
- Start tug game
- Say "drop it"
- If puppy releases → Resume play (play = reward)
- If puppy doesn't release → Game ends (timeout)

### Day 4 Success:
☐ Puppy waited for treat at least once
☐ One calm greeting achieved
☐ Released toy on "drop it" 2+ times

---

## DAY 5: Tired Puppy = Good Puppy

### Goal: Prevent Overtired Biting

**The #1 Cause of "Zoomies" and Biting Frenzies: Overtiredness**

**Enforced Nap Schedule:**
- 1 hour awake → 2 hours nap in crate
- Repeat all day
- Non-negotiable

**Signs Your Puppy Is Overtired:**
- ☐ Biting gets worse despite training
- ☐ Won't focus on toys
- ☐ Running in circles (zoomies)
- ☐ Whining or barking more

**Action:** Put puppy in crate immediately. Cover with blanket. Leave room.

**Mental Exercise = Physical Exhaustion:**
- 5 minutes training = 30 minutes walking
- Use food puzzles
- Practice commands
- Sniff walks (let them smell everything)

### Day 5 Challenge:
☐ Enforced 3 naps today
☐ One 10-minute training session
☐ Track: Was biting better after naps? ☐ Yes ☐ No

---

## DAY 6: Socialization (Carefully!)

### Goal: Learn Bite Inhibition from Other Dogs

**Best Teachers:** Well-mannered adult dogs

**Safe Socialization:**
- Puppy playdates with vaccinated, gentle adult dogs
- Adult dog will yelp/correct if puppy bites too hard
- This teaches bite inhibition naturally
- Supervise closely
- Separate if play gets too rough

**What Good Play Looks Like:**
- ☐ Both dogs take breaks
- ☐ Play bows (butt up, front down)
- ☐ Role reversing (chaser becomes chasee)
- ☐ Soft mouths, no yelping

**Red Flags:**
- ☐ One puppy always on bottom
- ☐ Continuous yelping
- ☐ Stiff bodies, raised hackles

### Day 6 Homework:
☐ Arranged one supervised play session
☐ Continued all techniques from Days 1-5
☐ Noticed improvement? ☐ Yes ☐ Somewhat ☐ No

---

## DAY 7: Review & Troubleshoot

### Goal: Assess Progress & Plan Next Steps

**Honest Assessment:**

1. **Frequency:** Biting attempts reduced by ___% (estimate)
2. **Intensity:** Bites are: ☐ Softer ☐ Same ☐ Harder
3. **Response:** Puppy stops when I say "ouch": ☐ Always ☐ Usually ☐ Sometimes ☐ Never

**If You're Seeing Improvement:** Keep going! Mastery takes 2-4 weeks of consistency.

**If You're NOT Seeing Improvement:**

**Common Problems & Solutions:**

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Everyone isn't consistent | Family meeting—create written rules |
| Puppy overtired | Enforce naps (1 up, 2 down) |
| Teething pain | Freeze wet washcloths, offer cold toys |
| Seeks attention through biting | Ignore completely, reward calm behavior |
| Bites harder when you yelp | Use timeout instead of verbal response |
| Only bites certain people | That person must be primary trainer |
| Aggressive biting (growling, stiff) | Consult vet/professional trainer immediately |

---

## Week 2 and Beyond

**Continue all techniques** - Puppies need 4-6 weeks of consistent training to fully internalize bite inhibition.

**Expected Timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Biting frequency reduces 30-50%
- **Week 2:** Biting intensity softens noticeably
- **Week 3-4:** Biting becomes rare, gentle mouthing only
- **Week 6-8:** Bite inhibition fully developed

**Adult Teeth = New Challenge:**
- Adult teeth come in at 4-7 months
- Brief regression common (teeth hurt!)
- Revisit this protocol if needed

---

## Emergency: When To Get Professional Help

**See a vet or certified dog trainer if:**
- ☐ Puppy over 6 months still biting hard
- ☐ Biting accompanied by growling, snarling
- ☐ Breaks skin regularly (beyond normal sharpness)
- ☐ Bites seem aggressive, not playful
- ☐ Guarding resources with bites
- ☐ No improvement after 4 weeks of consistent training
