## The Core 10 Commands

Master these and you have a well-behaved dog in any situation.

---

## 1. SIT

**What It Does:** Foundation for all other commands, impulse control
**When To Use:** Before meals, crossing streets, greeting people, vet visits

### How To Teach:
1. Hold treat at dog's nose
2. Slowly move treat up and back over head
3. Dog's butt naturally drops to floor
4. The INSTANT butt touches → "Yes!" + treat
5. Repeat 10 times before adding word "sit"

### Hand Signal:
Palm up, bring hand up toward chest

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Saying "sit" before dog knows what it means
- ❌ Pushing dog's butt down (they resist)
- ❌ Treating too slowly (must be instant)

### Mastery Level:
☐ Sits 90% on first cue
☐ Sits at distance (10 feet)
☐ Sits with distractions

---

## 2. DOWN

**What It Does:** Calm behavior, prevents jumping, emergency stop
**When To Use:** Restaurant patios, waiting rooms, guests visiting

### How To Teach:
1. Start from sit position
2. Hold treat at dog's nose
3. Slowly bring treat straight down to floor
4. Lure treat forward (between front paws)
5. When elbows touch floor → "Yes!" + treat

### Hand Signal:
Palm down, bring hand down toward floor

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Moving treat too fast
- ❌ Not bringing treat forward enough
- ❌ Expecting instant success (harder than sit)

### Progression:
Week 1: Down from sit
Week 2: Down from standing
Week 3: Down at distance
Week 4: Down-stay (hold position)

---

## 3. STAY

**What It Does:** Impulse control, safety (don't run out door)
**When To Use:** Door/gate opening, distractions, before releasing to play

### How To Teach:
1. Ask for sit or down
2. Hold palm up (stop sign)
3. Say "stay"
4. Count 3 seconds
5. Step back 1 step
6. Return to dog
7. Release with "okay!" + treat
8. Gradually increase time and distance

### Release Word:
CRITICAL: Always release with same word ("okay", "free", "break")
Never just walk away or dog learns to break stay

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Increasing distance before duration
- ❌ No clear release cue
- ❌ Calling dog to you from stay (breaks the command)

### Build It:
- **Duration:** 5 sec → 10 → 30 → 1 min → 3 min
- **Distance:** 1 step → 3 → 10 → 20 feet
- **Distractions:** Zero → mild → challenging

---

## 4. COME / RECALL

**What It Does:** MOST IMPORTANT safety command, off-leash control
**When To Use:** Dog gets loose, running toward danger, off-leash areas

### How To Teach:
1. Start indoors, zero distractions
2. Squat down, open arms, excited voice
3. Say "come!" or dog's name enthusiastically
4. When dog arrives → JACKPOT (handful of treats)
5. Repeat 10 times
6. Gradually add distance

### Golden Rules:
- ✅ NEVER call dog to punishment (nail trim, bath, etc.)
- ✅ NEVER call if you can't enforce (no leash = don't call until reliable)
- ✅ ALWAYS reward (even if they took 2 minutes)

### Proofing Protocol:
Week 1: Indoors, close distance
Week 2: Indoors, across room
Week 3: Outdoors, fenced yard on long-line
Week 4: Outdoors with mild distractions

### Emergency Recall (Advanced):
- Special word ONLY for emergencies ("here!", whistle)
- Never use casually
- ALWAYS jackpot reward
- Practice monthly to keep strong

---

## 5. LEAVE IT

**What It Does:** Prevents eating dangerous items, ignores distractions
**When To Use:** Food on ground, dead animals, other dogs, chasing squirrels

### How To Teach:
1. Treat in closed fist
2. Show fist to dog
3. Dog will paw, lick, mouth hand
4. Wait in silence
5. When dog backs off → Open hand + treat
6. Repeat 15 times
7. Add verbal "leave it" when they pull back

### Progression:
Level 1: Treat in hand
Level 2: Treat on floor (your foot on it)
Level 3: Treat on floor (no foot)
Level 4: Toss treat, say "leave it" mid-air

### Real-World Application:
- Food dropped while cooking
- Dead bird on walk
- Dog poop
- Trash on street

---

## 6. DROP IT

**What It Does:** Releases items from mouth, prevents resource guarding
**When To Use:** Has inappropriate item, tug games, found something dangerous

### How To Teach:
1. Dog has low-value toy in mouth
2. Show high-value treat near nose
3. Dog drops toy to eat treat
4. As they drop → Say "drop it"
5. Give treat AND return toy
6. This teaches: Dropping = rewards + getting item back

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Chasing dog (becomes keep-away game)
- ❌ Prying mouth open (creates resistance)
- ❌ Not returning item (dog learns to guard)

### Emergency Drop:
For dangerous items:
- Trade for REALLY good treat
- Give multiple treats
- Don't return dangerous item (but give toy instead)

---

## 7. WAIT / STAY AT DOORWAYS

**What It Does:** Prevents door bolting, safety
**When To Use:** Every single door, gate, car door

### How To Teach:
1. Approach door with dog
2. Ask for sit
3. Hand on door handle
4. If dog stands → Remove hand (try again)
5. When dog holds sit → Open door 1 inch
6. If dog stays sitting → Open fully
7. Say "okay" to release
8. Jackpot rewards

### Progression:
- Door opens 1 inch → 6 inches → fully open
- You step through → Walk 5 feet away → Out of sight
- Build until dog waits until released

**Life-Saving:** Prevents running into traffic, escaping yard

---

## 8. WATCH ME / FOCUS

**What It Does:** Gets dog's attention, foundation for all training
**When To Use:** Distractions, leash training, before giving any command

### How To Teach:
1. Treat at dog's nose
2. Bring treat to your eyes
3. When dog makes eye contact → "Yes!" + treat
4. Repeat 10 times
5. Add verbal "watch me" or "focus"
6. Build duration (1 sec → 3 → 5 → 10)

### Use Cases:
- Another dog approaching on walk
- Before crossing street
- Starting any training session
- Redirecting from distraction

---

## 9. OFF / NO JUMP

**What It Does:** Stops jumping on people, counters, furniture
**When To Use:** Greeting guests, countersurfing prevention

### How To Teach:
1. When dog jumps → Turn completely around
2. Cross arms, look away
3. Say nothing
4. Wait for 4 paws on floor
5. Turn back → If sitting, reward
6. If jumps again → Turn away again

### Consistent Rule:
**Jumping = all attention removed**
**4 paws on floor = pets and treats**

Everyone must follow this rule or it won't work.

### Alternative:
Teach "go to place" (mat/bed) when doorbell rings

---

## 10. PLACE / GO TO BED

**What It Does:** Sends dog to specific spot, settles excited behavior
**When To Use:** Guests arrive, mealtime, working from home, over-excitement

### How To Teach:
1. Use mat, dog bed, or towel as "place"
2. Lure dog onto place with treat
3. When all 4 paws on place → "Yes!" + treat
4. Release with "okay"
5. Repeat 10 times
6. Add distance: Send from 3 feet → 5 → 10
7. Add duration: Must stay on place 10 sec → 30 → 1 min

### Advanced:
- Dog goes to place when doorbell rings (automatic)
- Stays on place during meals
- Goes to place when you say "too much" (overstimulated)

**This command = your sanity saver**

## Bonus: Hand Signal Chart

Print and post where you train:

| Command | Verbal Cue | Hand Signal | Body Language |
|---------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Sit | "Sit" | Palm up, bring to chest | Stand tall |
| Down | "Down" | Palm down, move to floor | Lean slightly forward |
| Stay | "Stay" | Palm out (stop sign) | Stand still |
| Come | "Come" / Name | Pat legs, open arms | Squat, excited |
| Leave It | "Leave it" | Point at item, closed fist | Block with body if needed |
| Drop It | "Drop it" | Open palm under chin | Lean back (not forward) |
| Wait | "Wait" | Palm up at dog's face | Stand in doorway |
| Watch | "Watch" / "Focus" | Point to your eyes | Face dog directly |
| Off | "Off" | Push away motion (don't touch dog) | Turn body away |
| Place | "Place" / "Bed" | Point to location | Guide with leash if needed |

**Consistency = Key:** Everyone uses same cues and signals
