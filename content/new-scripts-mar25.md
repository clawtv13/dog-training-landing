## SCRIPT 8: MONEYSTACK - "The $6 Latte That Made Me $10,000"

**Duration:** 43 seconds
**Topic:** Micro-investing psychology trick
**Hook:** Counterintuitive intro

### **SCRIPT:**

```
[0:00-0:05]
Everyone told me to stop buying lattes. I bought more and got rich. Here's how.

[0:06-0:12]
Every time I bought a $6 latte, I opened my investing app and put $6 into the S&P 500.

[0:13-0:18]
It's called habit stacking. You link a bad habit with a good one. Your brain stops resisting.

[0:19-0:25]
After one year: I spent $1,500 on lattes. But I also invested $1,500. That's now worth $1,680.

[0:26-0:31]
Fast forward five years: That $7,500 turned into over $10,000 with compound interest.

[0:32-0:37]
I still got my lattes. But I also built wealth without feeling it.

[0:38-0:43]
Link any small spending habit to investing the same amount. Try it for 30 days.
```

### **WHY IT WORKS:**
- Flips the tired "skip the latte" advice on its head (pattern interrupt)
- Uses real numbers that feel achievable ($6/day)
- Introduces behavioral psychology (habit stacking) without jargon
- Shows that you don't have to sacrifice to save (permission-based motivation)
- Targets guilt around small purchases → turns it into wealth building

### **FILMING NOTES:**
- Props: Latte cup, phone showing investing app
- Visual: Split screen or text showing "$6 latte = $6 invested"
