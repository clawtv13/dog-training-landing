# Book 1 Research: The Killer Clown - Marlene Warren Murder

## Case Overview

**Victim:** Marlene Warren  
**Location:** Wellington, Florida  
**Date of Murder:** May 26, 1990  
**Resolution:** Arrested September 2017, convicted 2023  
**Years Unsolved:** 27 years  
**Killer:** Sheila Keen-Warren (later married victim's husband)

## The Crime

In 1990, Marlene Warren answered her door on a quiet Saturday morning in Wellington, Florida. Standing on her doorstep was someone dressed as a clown, holding colorful balloons and flowers. The clown shot her in the face at point-blank range, then calmly walked away and disappeared.

Witnesses saw the clown get into a white Chrysler LeBaron, but the costume made identification impossible. Police found the abandoned car, the costume, and the orange wig—but no solid leads.

## The Betrayal

While police searched for a random killer, the truth was hiding in plain sight. Marlene's husband, Michael Warren, was having an affair with **Sheila Keen**, an employee at his used car dealership. 

Witnesses reported seeing Sheila and Michael together before the murder. Someone matching Sheila's description purchased the clown costume and orange wig at a costume shop days before the murder. The white Chrysler matched a car registered in Sheila's name.

But police couldn't prove it. Sheila and Michael both denied involvement.

## The Aftermath

Two years after Marlene's murder, **Michael and Sheila married**. They moved to Virginia and lived openly as a couple for decades while the case went cold.

Marlene's son, Joseph Ahrens, never gave up. He pushed police to keep investigating. "They were at my mother's funeral," he said. "And then they got married."

## The Breakthrough

For 27 years, the orange wig sat in evidence. DNA testing in the 1990s was primitive and inconclusive. But by 2014, **DNA technology had advanced** dramatically.

New testing on fibers from the wig revealed DNA matches. In September 2017, police arrested Sheila Keen-Warren, now 54, in Virginia.

The case went to trial in 2023. Sheila accepted a plea deal rather than face life in prison. She was sentenced to 12 years but released in 2024 after time served.

## Key Elements

- **Killer Relationship:** Affair with victim's husband, later married him
- **Method:** Shot victim while disguised as clown with balloons
- **Forensic Breakthrough:** DNA from orange wig fibers
- **Years Unsolved:** 27 years
- **Betrayal Angle:** Attended funeral, married victim's husband
- **Justice:** Arrested 2017, convicted 2023

## Emotional Impact

Joseph Ahrens waited nearly three decades to see his mother's killer arrested. "She walked into the funeral and smiled at us," he recalled. "And two years later, she married my stepdad."
