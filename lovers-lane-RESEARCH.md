# The Lovers Lane Murders: Cheryl Henry and Andy Atkinson

## CASE SUMMARY

On August 22, 1990, Cheryl Henry (22) and Garland "Andy" Atkinson (21) went on a date at Bayou Mama's nightclub in Houston, Texas. They were last seen alive around 11:30 PM. The following day, August 23, 1990, a security guard discovered Atkinson's white Honda Civic parked in a secluded cul-de-sac in the 1300 block of Enclave Parkway in west Houston—an area known locally as "Lovers Lane" where young couples often parked.

Police found Henry's nude body partially concealed under wooden boards in a nearby wooded area. She had been sexually assaulted, bound with rope, and her throat slashed. Approximately 150-200 yards away, Atkinson's body was found tied to a tree, his hands bound behind his back, throat cut so deeply his head was nearly severed.

The case remained unsolved for nearly 36 years, becoming one of Houston's most notorious cold cases. On March 26, 2026, Floyd William Parrott (64) was arrested by FBI SWAT in Lincoln, Nebraska, and charged with two counts of capital murder. The breakthrough came from a tip received in late 2025 combined with DNA evidence linking Parrott to the crime through a 1996 sexual assault case that had recently been uploaded to CODIS. Harris County District Attorney Sean Teare stated authorities believe Parrott "preyed on this community for a long time" and may have additional victims.

## VICTIM PROFILE: Cheryl Henry

### Early Life & Family Background

Cheryl Henry was born on February 27, 1968, the eldest of seven children in a close-knit family. She grew up in the Fort Bend County area of greater Houston, Texas. Her mother was Barbara Craig. Cheryl was described as popular, outgoing, and full of life—someone who brought "much love and light" to those around her.

Her younger sister Shane Henry (later Shane Henry Blaine, then Shane Craig) was particularly close to Cheryl. The sisters had a bond that included regular expressions of love—kissing goodbye and saying "I love you" was routine between them. Shane would later become the family's spokesperson in seeking justice.

### Education & Activities

Cheryl was a member of the drill team at Dulles High School in Fort Bend County and later at Northwest Academy. Her involvement in drill team suggested discipline, coordination, and social engagement. She attended Kilgore Junior College before transferring to Stephen F. Austin State University in Nacogdoches, Texas, where she was enrolled at the time of her death.

### Personality & Interests

According to family members, Cheryl wasn't typically the type to get "worked up over a boy," which made her immediate, intense connection with Andy Atkinson notable. Her stepsister Cristl Craig observed that Cheryl "fell head over heels" for Andy in a way that was uncharacteristic for her.

She had a lively social presence and worked part-time at Rick's Cabaret, a gentlemen's club on Bering Street. This was not uncommon for young women in Houston at the time seeking flexible work with good earnings.

### Relationship with Andy Atkinson

Cheryl met Andy Atkinson during the summer of 1990 while home from college. They had known each other only about two weeks by the night of their final date. Despite the brief relationship, there was clear mutual infatuation. On the night of August 22, 1990, Shane observed the couple at Bayou Mama's nightclub and recalled jokingly telling them to "get a room" multiple times—noting it was during "that exciting time of a new relationship."

### The Last Night

On August 22, 1990, Cheryl met up with Andy and her sister Shane at Bayou Mama's nightclub on Westheimer. The three had drinks together, with Cheryl and Andy clearly smitten with each other. Around 11:30 PM, Shane said goodnight. "We kissed each other goodbye and told each other we loved each other, but that's what we always did," Shane later recalled. "And that's the last time I saw her."

## VICTIM PROFILE: Garland "Andy" Atkinson

### Early Life & Background

Garland Andrew "Andy" Atkinson was born circa 1969, making him 21 years old at the time of his death. He grew up with strong ties to family, particularly his father, Garland Atkinson Sr., who managed Dreams Street, a nightclub on Winrock in Houston.

"We were not only father and son but best friends as well," Garland Sr. later said. The elder Atkinson could not bring himself to go to the morgue to identify his son's body, later telling reporters: "You see things on TV, you read things in the paper, but you never imagine something that drastic, that horrifying, happening to someone you love."

### Education & Relocation

Andy attended Campbell University in North Carolina, completing at least one semester before deciding to relocate to Houston to be closer to his father's side of the family. This move demonstrated both his independence and his family orientation—willing to uproot his life to strengthen family bonds.

### Career & Work

After moving to Houston, Andy found work at Gold's Gym on the Gulf Freeway and moved in with his grandmother in the Meyerland neighborhood. He also occasionally worked the door at Dreams Street, his father's club on Winrock. This gave him familiarity with the Houston nightclub scene.

### Relationship with Cheryl

Andy had known Cheryl for approximately two weeks when they made plans for their date on August 22, 1990. The relationship was new but intense—witnesses noted their obvious affection for each other at Bayou Mama's nightclub that evening.

## THE CRIME

### Date, Time & Location

The murders occurred during the night of August 22-23, 1990. The crime scene was located in the 1300 block of Enclave Parkway (also referred to as Enclave Round) in west Houston, Harris County, Texas. At the time, this was an undeveloped business park—remote and largely wooded. The area was known locally as "Lovers Lane" due to its popularity among young couples seeking privacy.

Today, the Sysco Corporation building stands near the former crime scene.

### Method & Manner

Based on crime scene evidence and autopsy findings:

**Attack on Andy Atkinson:**
- Investigators believe Parrott approached the couple while they were in or near Atkinson's white Honda Civic
- Atkinson was tied up with hemp rope, hands bound behind his back
- He was marched approximately 50-200 yards from the vehicle into the wooded area
- He was tied to a tree in a seated position
- His throat was slashed with a knife—wounds were so deep his head was nearly severed
- He remained fully clothed

**Attack on Cheryl Henry:**
- Henry was also bound with rope, hands behind her back
- She was sexually assaulted
- Evidence in the vehicle (blood on driver's door handle, reclined seats, displaced floor mat) suggests some of the assault may have occurred in or near the car
- Her underwear was removed
- She was moved to a location in the woods approximately 150-200 yards from Atkinson
- Her throat was slashed with three ragged gashes
- Her nude body was left face-down and partially concealed under cedar fence slats "in what appeared to be an attempt to cover the body"

**Additional Details:**
- Items from Atkinson's car, including golf balls, were found "arranged in a way that drew attention toward Henry's location"
- A $20 bill was found nearby—investigators described this as unusual
- The key remained in the car's ignition
- The seats were reclined and a cassette tape was in the deck, suggesting the couple had been listening to music
- Police initially suspected multiple perpetrators due to the brutality

### Physical Evidence

- **DNA evidence:** Semen collected from Cheryl Henry during autopsy sexual assault examination
- **Vehicle evidence:** Blood on inside of driver's door near handle; rubber floor mat displaced; seats fully reclined; cassette tape in deck; key in ignition
- **Binding materials:** Hemp rope used to bind both victims' hands behind their backs
- **Crime scene staging:** Golf balls and other items arranged to point toward Henry's body; wooden fence slats used to cover body
- **June 1990 rape connection:** DNA from murder matched DNA from June 20, 1990 sexual assault of exotic dancer in north Harris County

### Cause of Death

Both victims died from sharp force injuries to the neck (throat slashing), likely inflicted with a knife. The wounds to Atkinson were particularly severe—his head was nearly severed.

## PERPETRATOR PROFILE: Floyd William Parrott

### Early Life & Background

Limited information is available about Floyd William Parrott's early life. He was born approximately 1961-1962, making him 28 years old at the time of the 1990 murders and 64 at his 2026 arrest. He lived in the Houston area for most of his life before relocating to Lincoln, Nebraska in approximately 2024.

### Criminal History & Pattern of Behavior

Parrott's documented criminal record reveals a disturbing pattern of impersonating law enforcement:

**1988 (May):** First arrest for impersonating a peace officer in Harris County. Placed on probation. Notably, at this time he provided a work address just over one mile from the future murder scene—an area then remote and undeveloped.

**1988 (December):** Arrested for carrying a weapon. Convicted. A blue steel revolver was later returned to him—this weapon matches the description given by the June 1990 rape victim.

**1990 (May):** Arrested again for impersonating a peace officer, specifically claiming to be a deputy constable for Precinct 4 and Precinct 5. Told at least one woman he was working as a security guard. Released on bond. **He was out on bond when both the June 1990 sexual assault and the August 1990 murders occurred.**

**1990 (July):** Five weeks before the Lovers Lane murders, arrested on two counts of impersonating a cop. Later received two-year sentence (served after the murders).

**1995 (September):** Sentenced to 20 years in prison for impersonating a public servant. In this case, he posed as a Montgomery County police chief, pulled over a driver on the Southwest Freeway near Chimney Rock using a car with emergency lights, and extorted $100 from the victim over an expired license plate. Police searched his home and found police uniforms, badges, and law enforcement equipment.

**1996:** While awaiting trial or on bond, accused of sexual assault. Victim was a woman who worked at a strip club. She declined to cooperate with prosecution due to not wanting to subject herself to cross-examination. Grand jury declined to indict, but DNA was collected. Parrott admitted sexual contact but claimed it was consensual.

Christopher Downey, the prosecutor in the 1995-96 case, later recalled: "Someone who impersonates a police officer and goes to the extent to solicit a bribe, when the whole time he was acting as a fake police officer, is pretty shocking... It was a pleasure to prosecute Floyd Parrott because we had reservations about his behavior—it was downright predatory."

### Employment & Lifestyle

Parrott worked as:
- Bouncer at nightclubs and strip clubs
- Employee at an auto parts store
- Possibly worked for a wrecker company

He owned property in New Caney, Texas, until 2020, then relocated to Lincoln, Nebraska within the past two years before his arrest.

### Psychological Profile

**Note:** No formal psychological evaluation from trial testimony is available as of this writing (case pending).

**Behavioral Patterns Observed:**

1. **Authority impersonation:** Repeated, compulsive impersonation of law enforcement over at least a decade (1988-1996, and reportedly into the 2000s per DA Teare). This suggests a need for power, control, and the ability to intimidate victims using perceived authority.

2. **Predatory targeting:** His victims included women in the adult entertainment industry—exotic dancers and club employees. Both Cheryl Henry (worked at Rick's Cabaret) and the June 1990 rape victim (worked at GiGi's) fit this profile. His employment as a bouncer at nightclubs and strip clubs gave him access to potential victims.

3. **Sexual sadism indicators:** The crime scene suggests sexual sadism—binding, sexual assault, throat-slashing of a female victim while her partner was forced to be nearby (possibly witnessing), and post-mortem staging of the scene.

4. **Organized offender characteristics:** Evidence suggests planning: familiarity with remote location, bringing binding materials and weapons, post-crime concealment of bodies, misdirection (arranged objects pointing to body).

DA Teare stated: "We feel really comfortable in saying that he's been preying on this community for a long time."

### Connection to Victims

Shane Craig (Cheryl's sister) stated the family knew nothing about Parrott and any connection he might have had to the victims: "We're all slowly trying to understand everything."

However, several circumstantial connections exist:
- Parrott's 1988 work address was approximately one mile from the murder scene
- Parrott worked as a bouncer at nightclubs/strip clubs; Cheryl worked at Rick's Cabaret; Andy occasionally worked the door at Dreams Street (his father's club)
- The June 1990 rape victim (whose assault is DNA-linked to the murders) had once worked for Andy's father, Garland Atkinson Sr.

Investigators in 2007 noted: "Police didn't know if the killer might also have worked for Garland Atkinson or if it was just a crazy coincidence."

## THE INVESTIGATION

### Initial Response

On August 23, 1990, after Cheryl failed to appear at work, her family filed a missing persons report at approximately 5:00 PM. That evening, a security guard conducting routine patrol discovered Atkinson's white Honda Civic parked in an isolated cul-de-sac. The car had been stationary for hours. The guard noted a purse on the passenger-side floorboard containing Henry's identification.

Houston Police responded. Search teams including patrol officers, cadaver dogs, helicopters, and mounted police combed the partially wooded, undeveloped area. The night was hot, muggy, and insect-ridden. Family members gathered anxiously at the scene.

### Key Investigators

- **Detective John "Billy" Belk** - Primary investigator for 19 years (1990-2007). Legendary commitment to the case. Retired in 2007, the week after learning of the DNA breakthrough linking the June 1990 rape to the murders.
- **Detective Michael Miller** - Inherited case from Belk. Conducted 2007-2008 investigation following DNA match.
- **Detective Michael Burrow** - Joined HPD cold case unit January 2026. Credited with bringing "fresh eyes" to review of thousands of pages; identified Parrott as suspect.
- **Samantha Knecht** - Lead attorney, Harris County DA cold-case division. Worked on case for over a decade.
- **Harris County DA Sean Teare** - Announced charges; led 2026 press conference.
- **FBI Houston** - Joint investigation; FBI SWAT conducted arrest in Nebraska.

### Breakthrough Moments

**2007 - First DNA Link:**
When Harris County Sheriff's Office began processing a backlog of old rape kits, DNA from the June 20, 1990 exotic dancer rape was matched to DNA from Henry's body. This confirmed the same perpetrator committed both crimes. A composite sketch was released. However, the suspect remained unidentified.

**2025/2026 - The Tip and CODIS Match:**
In late 2025, HPD received a tip naming Floyd William Parrott. Detective Burrow investigated, discovered the 1996 sexual assault case, and learned that DNA from that case had "recently" been uploaded to CODIS. The database registered a case-to-case match between:
- DNA from 1996 assault (Parrott admitted sexual contact)
- DNA from June 1990 exotic dancer rape
- DNA from Cheryl Henry's autopsy (1990 murder)

### Critical Question: Why the 19-Year Gap?

The most confounding aspect of this investigation is the timeline:
- **2007:** DNA from June 1990 rape matched to 1990 murder
- **2026:** Arrest finally made

The 1996 sexual assault DNA was described as "recently placed" into CODIS. It remains unclear:
- Why Parrott's DNA from the 1996 case wasn't already in the database
- Why investigators kept DNA samples for decades after that prosecution was dropped
- Whether the 2007 DNA match to June 1990 rape could have led to Parrott sooner

DA Teare declined to answer questions about this gap. The tipster who named Parrott in late 2025 proved crucial—without that tip, it's unclear when or if the CODIS match would have been pursued.

### Evidence Collection Challenges

- Over 100 people were looked at over 36 years
- At least two dozen potential suspects had DNA compared—all cleared
- Parrott was **never** among those questioned before 2026
- The killer left DNA (semen) at scene, but pre-CODIS limitations and database gaps prevented identification

## LEGAL PROCEEDINGS

### Arrest

**Date:** March 25-26, 2026
**Location:** Lincoln, Nebraska
**Circumstances:** FBI SWAT team executed arrest. Parrott was taken into custody and transported to Lancaster County Jail in Nebraska.

During custodial interview on March 26, Parrott allegedly denied ever meeting Cheryl Henry.

### Charges Filed

**Charge:** Capital Murder (two counts)
**Court:** 176th Criminal District Court, Harris County, Texas
**Filing Date:** March 26, 2026

Capital murder in Texas applies when a defendant commits murder during the commission of another felony (here, sexual assault) or kills multiple victims in the same criminal episode.

### Current Status (as of April 1, 2026)

- Parrott remains in Nebraska custody awaiting extradition to Harris County Jail
- DA Teare indicated prosecutors will seek to hold Parrott without bail upon transfer to Texas
- No trial date has been set
- No formal plea has been entered

### Potential Penalties

Capital murder in Texas is punishable by:
- Death penalty, OR
- Life imprisonment without possibility of parole

## PSYCHOLOGY & MOTIVE ANALYSIS

### Established Motive

The prosecution has not formally stated a motive as of this writing (pre-trial). However, evidence suggests:

**Sexual predation:** The sexual assault of Cheryl Henry, combined with DNA links to at least two other sexual assaults (June 1990, August 1996), indicates serial sexual offending as primary motivation.

**Power and control:** Parrott's repeated impersonation of law enforcement—complete with uniforms, badges, emergency lights, and weapons—demonstrates an obsession with wielding authority over others. This extended to pulling over drivers and extorting money.

**Target selection:** Victims connected to adult entertainment industry. Parrott's employment as nightclub bouncer provided access. His victims may have been selected based on perceived vulnerability or availability.

### Psychological Factors

**Warning Signs (documented):**
- Compulsive law enforcement impersonation (1988-2000s)
- Serial sexual offending (at least 3 victims linked by DNA)
- Escalation: from impersonation → armed robbery under color of authority → rape → murder
- "Downright predatory behavior" noted by prosecutor who dealt with him in 1996

**Possible Serial Killer Indicators:**
- DA Teare stated: "We feel really comfortable in saying that he's been preying on this community for a long time."
- "If you recognize this individual and he pulled you over... call us. If you met him once, if you met him at a club, if you knew him at all, reach out."
- "We know there are other victims," Detective Miller stated in 2008.

### Alternative Theories

Prior to Parrott's identification, investigators considered:
- The killer might have worked for Garland Atkinson Sr. (Andy's father), given the June 1990 rape victim's employment connection
- Multiple perpetrators (due to crime's brutality)
- Someone with familiarity to the specific location

It remains unclear whether Parrott had any connection to Garland Atkinson Sr.'s businesses or if the overlapping connections were coincidental.

### Media Coverage

The Lovers Lane Murders became one of Houston's most enduring unsolved crimes:
- Subject of regular newspaper retrospectives on anniversaries
- Featured on television true crime programs
- Crime Junkie podcast episode "INFAMOUS: Lovers' Lane Murders" (August 2025)
- Library Tales Publishing released book: "The Lover's Lane Murders: The Tragic Story of Cheryl Henry and Andy Atkinson"

### Family Advocacy

The Henry and Atkinson families maintained public pressure for decades:
- Shane Henry (Cheryl's sister) became primary family spokesperson
- Participated in news stories and interviews
- Attended 2026 press conference announcing arrest

Tragically, both Cheryl's mother Barbara Craig and Andy's father Garland Atkinson Sr. passed away in 2024—just one year before the arrest.

"It's heartbreaking that they don't get to have this day, but we know they're with them and rejoicing in their own ways," Shane Henry said.

### Impact on Investigation

Retired Detective Billy Belk, who worked the case for 19 years, was contacted the day of the arrest while at the dentist. He rushed downtown to share the news with the family:

"This is a good day. I want to thank you guys, because you all kept it alive for almost 36 years. I kept telling you this case would clear. I know it's hard to have patience."

### Call for Additional Victims

DA Teare made a public appeal for others who may have encountered Parrott:

"We believe he's been preying on this community for a long time... If you recognize this individual and he pulled you over, if he impersonated an officer, call us. If you met him once, if you met him at a club, if you knew him at all, reach out."
