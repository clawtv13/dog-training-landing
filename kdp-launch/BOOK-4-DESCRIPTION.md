## Description (HTML format for KDP)

```html
<b>On July 27, 1990, Dr. Ed Friedland came home to find his wife dead on their bedroom floor.</b>

Kim Thomas Friedland was 32 years old. She was a women's rights activist, a devoted mother, and a fierce advocate for justice. Someone strangled her in broad daylight in a safe Charlotte neighborhood, and for 35 years, her killer walked free.

<b>The wrong man was charged. A civil jury found the real killer liable. And DNA technology had to be invented before justice could arrive.</b>

This is the true story of:
• A passionate activist murdered in her own home
• A husband wrongfully accused and vindicated by science
• A neighbor named in a 1990 police report—living five minutes away
• An $8.6 million civil verdict that couldn't put anyone in prison
• The DNA breakthrough that finally proved what a jury already knew

<b>From the author of the MIND CRIMES series:</b>

<i>The Activist's Murder</i> is a story of science catching up to truth, of a woman who fought for others and deserved justice herself, and of a 35-year wait that should never have happened.

<b>Kim Thomas Friedland was killed on July 27, 1990. On February 19, 2026, Marion Gales was arrested. This is how DNA gave her justice—35 years too late.</b>
```
