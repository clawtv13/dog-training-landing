# Amazon Book Description - "The Girl in the Ditch: The April Tinsley Murder"

**For thirty years, a killer taunted police with his own DNA. He left notes on bicycles. He mailed cryptic messages. He wanted them to catch him—but they couldn't.**

**Until science finally caught up.**

---

On April 1, 1988, eight-year-old **April Tinsley** walked three blocks to retrieve her umbrella—the yellow one with cartoon characters she'd saved three weeks' allowance to buy. She never made it home. Three days later, a jogger found her body in a ditch twenty miles from Fort Wayne, Indiana. She'd been raped, strangled, and discarded like trash.

For thirty years, April's killer lived free. No arrests. No suspects. Just DNA sitting in an evidence locker, waiting for technology to catch up. Then, in 2004, **the killer returned**. He left taunting notes on little girls' bicycles—complete with his own DNA. He *wanted* to be caught. But police still couldn't identify him. He had no criminal record. No fingerprints in the system. He was a **ghost**.

Until 2018, when a revolutionary forensic technique called **genetic genealogy** changed everything. The same technology that caught the Golden State Killer finally gave April's family the answer they'd waited three decades to hear: **"We got him."**

**This is the story of:**
- An 8-year-old girl who trusted a stranger
- A 30-year cold case that haunted an entire city
- A killer who taunted police—and left the evidence that would convict him
- The DNA breakthrough that made the invisible visible
- A mother who never stopped fighting for justice

**"The Girl in the Ditch"** is a gripping true crime account of tragedy, resilience, and the science that finally brought a monster to justice. If you loved *I'll Be Gone in the Dark* or *The Five*, you'll be unable to put this down.
