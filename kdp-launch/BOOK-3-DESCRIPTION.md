# Book Description: The Lovers Lane Murders

**JUST SOLVED — MARCH 2026**

Five days ago, FBI SWAT teams surrounded a quiet house in Lincoln, Nebraska. Inside: **Floyd William Parrott**, a 64-year-old man who'd been living under the radar for decades. He's now charged with **capital murder** in a case that has haunted Houston for **36 years**. 

This is the **first book** to tell the complete story of how he almost got away with it.

**AUGUST 23, 1990.** Cheryl Henry (22) and Andy Atkinson (21) drove to a remote spot in west Houston that locals called "Lovers Lane." They were on a date. They'd known each other two weeks. They were falling in love.

By sunrise, both were dead—**bound to trees, throats slashed, left in the woods** like some twisted ritual killing. The brutality shocked Houston. The case consumed the city. Over 100 suspects were investigated. Two dozen DNA tested. All cleared.

For 36 years, the killer walked free.

**HERE'S THE TWIST:** Floyd Parrott was **named TWICE** as a suspect—once in 1990, again in 1996—but never arrested. He was a **serial police impersonator** who used fake badges to prey on women. He'd been arrested **five times** for impersonating cops before the murders. His DNA was collected in a 1996 sexual assault case—**but never uploaded to the database** until 2025.

**WHY DID IT TAKE 36 YEARS?**

In 2007, a DNA match linked the Lovers Lane killer to another rape—a woman attacked two months *before* Cheryl and Andy were murdered. Same predator. Same M.O. Uniform-style clothing. Long-barreled revolver. Fake authority used to control and terrorize.

But the suspect's name remained unknown. For **19 more years**, the case sat cold while the killer lived 800 miles away, believing he was untouchable.

Then, in late 2025, an **anonymous tip** named Floyd Parrott. A cold case detective with fresh eyes found a buried 1996 report. DNA was finally uploaded. Three cases—**one match**. June 1990 rape. August 1990 double murder. August 1996 sexual assault.

All Floyd William Parrott.

**MARCH 25, 2026:** Arrested at dawn. Charged with capital murder. DNA doesn't lie.

**"We believe he's been preying on this community for a long time,"** said Harris County DA Sean Teare at the March 27 press conference. Investigators now believe there are **more victims**—women who were pulled over by a fake cop in the 1980s and 1990s, women who never came forward, women who filed reports that were ignored.

This case exposes **catastrophic system failures:** A killer named twice. DNA collected but not uploaded. Arrests for impersonating cops—yet no one connected the dots. Cheryl and Andy's families waited 36 years. Cheryl's mother and Andy's father both **died in 2024**, never knowing who murdered their children.

**"This case will clear," promised Detective Billy Belk, who worked it for 19 years before retiring in 2007.** He was right. But it took one anonymous tip, one determined sergeant, and **DNA that sat in an evidence locker for 30 years** before justice finally came.

**THIS IS BREAKING NEWS TRUE CRIME.** The arrest happened days ago. The trial is coming. You're reading the **first book** on a case the world is just now discovering.

**READ IT NOW** before the trial begins—and before this predator's full story is forgotten again.
