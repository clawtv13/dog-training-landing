## Description (HTML format for KDP)

```html
<b>On September 1, 1984, 22-year-old Sandra Kaye Davis was found strangled beside a home in Waycross, Georgia.</b>

She was sexually assaulted. The DNA samples were collected. And then... nothing. For forty-two years.

Technology didn't exist to read the evidence. Detectives retired. Sandra's parents died without answers. Her siblings gave up hope. The case sat cold in a filing cabinet—longer than Sandra had been alive.

<b>Until March 31, 2026.</b>

This is the story of:
• A small-town murder that shocked Waycross in 1984
• DNA evidence that waited forty-two years for science to catch up
• A family who buried Sandra and then buried their hope
• Detective Marcus Reed, who refused to let the case die
• Freddie Lee Granger Jr.—age 68—arrested for a crime committed when he was 26

<b>From the author of the MIND CRIMES series:</b>

<i>Justice After 42 Years</i> is a breaking-news true crime account of genetic genealogy, probabilistic genotyping, and a brother who thought he'd never see justice. It's about patience, science, and the truth waiting in evidence lockers.

<b>Sandra Kaye Davis was murdered on September 1, 1984. Forty-two years later—longer than she'd been alive—DNA gave her justice. This is how.</b>
```
